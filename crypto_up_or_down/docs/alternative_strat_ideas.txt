2. Cross-Market Arbitrage
(Huge inefficiency)

Polymarket often lists multiple markets that must sum to 100%.

Example:

Market A:
BTC above 80k by March 31

Market B:
BTC above 85k by March 31

Logical constraint:

P(>85k) ≤ P(>80k)

But sometimes:

>80k = 0.40
>85k = 0.38

This implies:

P(80k–85k) = 2%

Which is often wrong.

Trade structure

Buy:

YES >80k
NO >85k

This synthetically creates:

BTC between 80k and 85k

Often priced incorrectly.

Why it exists

Polymarket markets are priced independently.

There is no market maker enforcing consistency.

3. Resolution Time Decay
(Massive retail mistake)

Probability changes non-linearly as time approaches.

Retail traders assume linear decay.

Example:

Market:

BTC above 80k by Friday

Thursday night:

price = 79.5k

Retail says:

50%

But actual probability depends on:

remaining volatility
hours remaining

Which may be:

32%
Signal

Compute:

model_probability(t)

Then trade when:

|model − market| > threshold
Best markets

Short expiry markets:

<72 hours
4. Liquidity Shock Lag
(very real)

When crypto moves fast, Polymarket lags Binance.

Example:

BTC jumps:

+4% in 10 minutes

Prediction markets adjust slowly.

Retail traders update manually.

Trade

Detect rapid price move:

BTC move > 2%

Then recompute probability.

Trade mispriced markets.

Typical edge
5–20% temporary mispricing
Duration
2–10 minutes
Hardware needed

None.

Speed matters much less than HFT.

5. Correlated Market Arbitrage

Polymarket often lists related markets.

Example:

BTC above 80k this month
BTC above 80k this year

Logical constraint:

P(month) ≤ P(year)

Sometimes violated.

Another example:

ETH > $5k this year
BTC > $100k this year

These events are correlated.

But markets price them independently.

Trade

Build correlation model:

ρ(BTC, ETH)

Then detect inconsistent probabilities.

Example

Market says:

BTC > 100k = 60%
ETH > 5k = 20%

But historically ETH moves with BTC.

That may be inconsistent.

The Single Biggest Polymarket Inefficiency

This one is much bigger than people realize.

Structural Inefficiency: Retail Probability Bias

Retail traders systematically misprice tail probabilities.

They assume price distributions look like this:

normal distribution

But crypto actually looks like:

fat-tailed distribution

Meaning:

extreme moves happen far more often
What retail believes
P(BTC +30% in a month) ≈ 2%
Reality

Historically closer to:

10–15%
Result

Polymarket often dramatically underprices extreme outcomes.

Example market:

BTC > $120k this year

Market price:

3%

Model probability:

8%
Trade

Always scan for:

long-tail events

Examples:

BTC > huge number
ETH > huge number
volatility spikes
crypto ETF approvals
Why this works

Human psychology hates extreme outcomes.

People underweight:

rare but possible events

This is a well documented behavioral bias.
